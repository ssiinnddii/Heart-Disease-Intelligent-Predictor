Understanding Heart Health

The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body, delivering oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removing waste products. Keeping your heart healthy is essential for overall wellbeing.

Heart disease is a broad term that includes several conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. Risk may increase due to factors such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, physical inactivity, obesity, stress, and family history.

Common Heart Conditions
  • Coronary artery disease
  • Heart attack
  • Heart failure
  • Arrhythmias
  • High blood pressure
  • Valve disease
Key Risk Factors

High cholesterol, high blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, obesity, unhealthy diet, and low physical activity.

Common Warning Signs

Chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, fatigue, palpitations, and discomfort in the arm, neck, or jaw.

Prevention Tips

Maintain a balanced diet, exercise regularly, avoid smoking, manage stress, and monitor blood pressure and cholesterol.
